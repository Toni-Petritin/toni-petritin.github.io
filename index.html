Tabletop RPG Rule & World Design

Although all of these were hobby projects rather than commercial ones, I still consider them worth mentioning as they are full rulesets and worlds designed by me from the ground up.

I learned a considerable amount about numerical game design balancing, narrative design, and player experience from these projects.

Key Skills Developed

  • World-building and lore creation
  • Game mechanics and rule design
  • Numerical balancing and playtesting
  • Use of tools like spreadsheets and custom software for design

Notable Projects

A narrative-focused RPG that approaches storytelling with a similar take as FateCore.

Project report link.

"For fun", but I took few project hours for school, which is why the report is also more in-depth.

I went with a sci-fi theme, which posed a number of new challenges. These challenges included a far more vast item list than I have usually needed, and a more varied list of organizations and factions to make the world more believable in a far more connected interstellar environment.

A horror rpg set in a fictional late nineteenth century era world called Riverside.

The rule set went through a couple of iterations during development and testing. This was due to the system being very tightly specced to model a very specific type of combat and skill usage to accommodate the horror theme.

Divine Wars is a fantasy roleplaying game I designed to span over countless worlds ruled by a selection of near godlike species of monsters. The players themselves play members of one of these godlike species - there are several selections, but all players canonically have to be from the same species per campaign.

The system functioned predominantly on top of a foundational set of rules, which each species altered somewhat to accommodate their individual power levels and skill sets. Subsequently each of these altered rulesets were called by a different name based on the species theme. The title "Divine Wars" is an umbrella term I made up to cover all these variations.

This was my very first full rpg I designed from the ground up with its own rules and world.

Third World is a relatively standard fantasy rpg set in a world where two groups of gods had been exiled after their attempted rebellion against the greater gods.

None of the original design documents have survived the test of time. The rulesets were clunky, but functional. The document for the world desing was around 40-50 pages long.
